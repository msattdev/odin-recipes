Gnocchi Soup

Back to Recipes

Description

Imagine a soup that is a hug in a bowl, a creamy and comforting masterpiece that warms you from the inside out. This is that gnocchi soup. At the heart of this recipe is a velvety, rich broth, a savory foundation built from a gentle simmer of aromatic vegetables like sweet carrots, tender celery, and mild onions. A touch of garlic and a whisper of fragrant herbs like thyme and rosemary weave their way through, creating a captivating aroma that will fill your kitchen and beckon everyone to the table. Swimming in this luscious, cream-kissed broth are the stars of the show: pillowy, tender potato gnocchi. These soft, delicate dumplings are like little bites of heaven, soaking up the flavorful soup and offering a delightful, melt-in-your-mouth texture in every spoonful.But the symphony of flavors and textures doesn't end there. Wilted spinach or kale adds a beautiful ribbon of color and a wholesome earthiness, while optional additions like savory Italian sausage or tender, shredded chicken can transform this into a truly hearty and satisfying one-pot meal. Each bite is a delightful interplay of creamy, savory, and tender, a culinary experience that is both elegant and deeply comforting.Finished with a generous sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan cheese that melts lazily into the warm soup and a grinding of black pepper, this gnocchi soup is more than just a recipeâ€”it's an invitation to slow down, savor the moment, and indulge in pure, delicious comfort.


Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp Olive Oil
  • 1 tbsp Butter
  • 1 lb Boneless, Skinless Chicken Breasts, cooked and shredded or diced (a rotisserie chicken works beautifully for a shortcut!)
  • 1 cup Yellow Onion, finely chopped
  • 2/3 cup Celery, finely chopped
  • 2/3 cup Carrots, shredded or finely chopped
  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Dried Thyme
  • 6 tbsp All-Purpose Flour
  • Salt and Freshly Ground Black Pepper, to taste
  • 4 cups Chicken Broth, low sodium recommended
  • 2 cups Half-and-Half (or 1 cup milk + 1 cup heavy cream for a richer soup)
  • 1 (16-ounce) package Potato Gnocchi (shelf-stable or refrigerated)
  • 3 cups Fresh Spinach, roughly chopped
  • Freshly Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • Red Pepper Flakes

Steps

  1. Place a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the olive oil and butter.
  2. Once the butter has melted, add the chopped onion, celery, and carrots. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables have softened and the onion is translucent.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and dried thyme. Cook for another minute until fragrant. Be careful not to let the garlic burn.
  4. Sprinkle the all-purpose flour over the softened vegetables. Stir continuously for about 1-2 minutes to cook out the raw flour taste. This mixture will be thick and pasty.
  5. Gradually pour in the chicken broth, whisking constantly as you add it. Pouring slowly and whisking will prevent any lumps from forming. Continue to whisk until the broth is smooth.
  6. Bring the soup to a gentle simmer. Let it cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, allowing the broth to thicken nicely.
  7. Reduce the heat to low and slowly stir in the half-and-half. Continue to stir until it's well combined. Do not let the soup boil after adding the dairy, as it can cause it to curdle.
  8. Stir the cooked, shredded chicken into the soup.
  9. Add the potato gnocchi directly to the pot. Stir gently.
  10. Let the soup simmer over medium-low heat for 3-5 minutes, or until the gnocchi are cooked through and have floated to the top. They will be plump and tender.
  11. Turn off the heat. Stir in the chopped fresh spinach. The residual heat from the soup will wilt it perfectly in just a minute or two.
  12. Taste the soup and season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper until the flavors are just right.
  13. Ladle the hot soup into bowls.
  14. Garnish with a sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan cheese, a pinch of red pepper flakes if you like some heat, or some chopped fresh parsley.
  15. Serve immediately with a side of warm, crusty bread for dipping into that incredible creamy broth.
