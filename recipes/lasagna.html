Lasagna

Back to Recipes

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that is loved by many for its rich flavors and comforting layers. At its core, lasagna consists of sheets of pasta layered with a savory meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and a generous amount of cheese. The dish is baked until bubbly and golden, creating a deliciously satisfying meal that is perfect for feeding a crowd or enjoying as leftovers.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef or sausage over medium heat until browned. Drain excess fat.
  4. Add the marinara sauce to the skillet and simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, basil, salt, and pepper.
  6. To assemble the lasagna, spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  7. Layer 4 noodles over the sauce, then spread half of the ricotta mixture on top.
  8. Add a layer of meat sauce, followed by a layer of mozzarella cheese.
  9. Repeat the layers, finishing with a layer of noodles and sauce on top.
  10. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese over the top.
  11. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until cheese is bubbly and golden.
  12. Let cool for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
